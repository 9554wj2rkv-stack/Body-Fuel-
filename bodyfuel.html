Reading a label
  • Check the serving size first — every number is per serving.
  • Ingredients are listed by weight; the first few make up most of the food.
  • More protein and fibre usually means a more filling food.
  • Long lists of unfamiliar names usually mean more processing.

2 · Carb

3 · Fat
Add a measured amount of oil or butter — small amounts count quickly.
4 · Veg / salad
Add vegetables or salad for fibre and volume — or choose none.

Weigh & Learn

Weigh your food now and then to learn what real portions look like. This is about awareness, not strict calorie counting.

Protein
Usually 100–150g cooked meat or fish — about a palm.
Carbs
Usually 100–200g cooked rice or potatoes — more on training days, less on rest days.
Fat
Oil and butter are dense. 1 tbsp olive oil ≈ 120 kcal — measure it rather than free-pouring.
